# Long-Island-Coffee-Website
Website from scratch project BCS350

--------------------------------------------------
PART 1: CONTENT (Answer ALL questions, add your own if necessary)

1. What is the name of your business?
	Long Island Coffee 
2. Is the business real or fictional?
	It is a fictional coffee shop
3. What products or services does the business offer?
	It serves caffeine products and snacks
4. Who is the target audience?
	The target audience is young people and college students
5. What problem does this business solve?
Generic Long Island Coffee Shop provides a comfy, community space for young people to have coffee, and a place to work on assignments 
6. What makes this business unique compared to competitors?
It will have locally roasted coffee, ethically sourced coffee, and snacks made in-house. It will also have art on the walls and a nice study area
7. What pages will your website include (e.g., Home, About, Services, Contact)?
	The pages will be the home, about us, menu, and contact information pages.
8. Write a short mission statement for the business
The mission of Long Island Coffee Shop is to bring people together, give them comfort, and provide them with ethical and locally roasted and brewed coffee
9. What tone should the website content have (professional, friendly, modern, etc.)?
	The tone will be friendly and modern, it will be welcoming but not too unprofessional
10. What call-to-action do you want visitors to take?
	Some call-to-actions will an about us page, 
11. What information must be immediately visible on the homepage?
	Information immediately visible will be a logo, name, 
12. Will the site include images, videos, or other media? Why?
	The site will include images of the coffee, the menu, and the logo of the business

--------------------------------------------------
PART 2: DESIGN (Answer ALL questions, add your own if necessary)

1. What overall style will the website have (minimalist, corporate, creative, etc.)?
	The style will be clean, cozy
2. What color scheme will you use and why?
	I will use brown(#4a2c2a), cream(#F0DEBB), gold(#BE9757), and some gray(474441) for the text
3. What fonts will you use for headings and body text?
	Yanone Kaffeesatz is a  great font for a Coffee shop website
4. How will spacing and layout improve readability?
Padding between text will make it easier for readers to pay attention, and line spacing will help make the text look less compact
6. What visual hierarchy will guide the user’s attention?
I’ll use large headings at the top, have smaller headings between sections, and have buttons with colors that pop out compared to the background. Also, put the more important information at the top of the pages.
7. How will consistency be maintained across pages?
	The same colors, fonts, spacing, and layout will be used for each section. 
8. What icons will you use?
	I’ll use coffee cups and beans
9. What emotions should the design evoke in users?
	The emotions will be cozy, secure, and peaceful
10. How will accessibility be considered (contrast, font size, etc.)?
Make a contrast between the text color and the background color
11. What inspiration sources influenced your design?
Looking through many coffee shop designs, and imagining what I would like to see in a coffee website. I also make the colors make it look like a stereotypical coffee cup design.

12. Describe the layout of the homepage and build the wireframe
I’ll make a top bar with the different areas of the website and the logo/title, fill space with a little slogan area, have an about us section, a menu, address and time open, and a section for user interaction

1. What interactive elements will your site include (forms, buttons, menus, etc.)?
	Buttons

	There will be a top bar that will guide them to the different sections
3. Will there be form validation or user feedback? Explain.
	I'll have a contact us section on the website

	I’ll find out how to make clicking buttons scroll to the section, and a part for interaction

It makes the website feel smoother, and makes it feel more personal by having feedback.
